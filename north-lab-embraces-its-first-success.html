LUXEMBOURG / Feb 01, 2020

North embraces its first success

Banque Internationale à Luxembourg R&D department, which launched last year, announced today that 24, a credit consumer app, will continue as spin-off.

BIL aims at staying ahead of its competitors embracing digital technologies though an innovation mindset. Investing in North in 2019 allowed the 1856 established Luxembourg-based bank to increase the pace of innovation and to be much closer to its customer expectations. Following an internal start at BIL, the 24 credit consumer tool will continue as a standalone brand offering loans to all European households and individuals, not just BIL customers.

Most companies that get disrupted are generally well-run companies with excellent conventional management practices.
BIL was one of them.

A decade ago digital banking was perceived as a modern “nice-to-have”, but its acceptance came faster than some were expecting. New regulation and open banking emphasized those changes and threats. FinTech were testing new business models every day and there was no more doubt BIL had to develop and test new concepts if it wanted to keep its head out of water.
Reminding that great companies such as Kodak, BlackBerry or Nokia were unable to perform some innovation during their good times, BIL didn’t want to face the same fate.

BIL had to fund the innovations that would deliver tomorrow’s value. The main issue was that even if it felt it should be spending more on innovation, the battle between today operational issues and tomorrow new opportunities raged on. Unfortunately, tomorrow opportunities generally lose and no budget is allocated to new great ideas.

While transformation of the financial scene itself represent many challenges, bank executives saw this as a huge growth opportunity. As a consequence, BIL stopped thinking as if it were a single monolithic organization with one business model and embraced a new way of considering innovation. It decided to favor a mix between cash cow products (retail and private banking) and a portfolio of new products that were searching for a profitable business model.

After having launched in 2016 its Business Transformation plan, the bank decided (2 months only after Lenovo’s holdings acquired Precision Capital’s stake – coincidence?) to go one step further and setup its R&D department, North Lab.

In the first stage we were thinking that projects incubated within the Lab would be a sandbox for BIL. This is not what happened! Actually, BIL and Luxembourg were used by 24 as a proof of concept. It can now stand on its own feet and target 160M potential European millennials!
David Mansy, Innovation Lab driver

Consumer credit was the first topic covered by the Lab. An in dept analysis had already been done and BIL wanted to digitalize this service. As a result, Lab worked side by side with the XCCX (Cross Chanel Customer Experience) team.
It was a great success: adopting a Lean Startup mindset and an Agile methodology, they built a first prototype and an MVP in 3 weeks. Iterating on a build-test-learn loop, the feature went live in less than 4 months. They got ready for the Auto Festival (a period during which car dealers have special promotions for people looking to purchase new vehicles and where banks offer tempting loans to finance them) and BIL registered 826 new loans (out of 5,184 cars sold) over the 10 day period!

Built on this success, BIL decided to improve the process and the 24 project was born. 24 can instantly pre-approve or decline any loan request. Once pre-approved, customers can upload their documents and the final decision is made within 24 hours. 24 technology has then been implemented in BILnet App in 2 weeks.

Being a FinTech which is trade mark of a bank like BIL is really helpful because it brings trust and security in customers mind.
Nathalie Knops, Business Transformation plan driver

Seeing the growth potential, BIL decided to provide 24 with more resources and decided to create a standalone trade mark which is now targeting a new customer segment (far from old fashion HNWI), European millennials.
Those creative, idealistic, and hyper-connected individuals needed a fresh brand, with a strong corporate culture, and which offered a unique experience, authenticity and a good value.
Talking about value, Starling, one of the most successful FinTech, has just contacted 24: they want 24’s offer to be onboarded on their marketplace!
The company is doing well and its future is looking bright.

Let’s take Revolut or N26 example: within 3 to 5 years they became unicorns. Who knowns, 24 should become more valuable than BIL.
Aurélien Dobbels, 24 driver
